## Creative Performance

### Top Performers

1. **Testimonial Card — Mom Review** — 5.07x ROAS, $19.74 CPA, 38 conversions. Highest efficiency in the account. CTR of 4.22% is strong. Frequency at 1.6 means there's significant headroom to scale spend before fatigue sets in. The social proof angle is clearly resonating with the target audience.

2. **Before After — 30 Day Results** — 4.07x ROAS, $24.59 CPA, 61 conversions (highest volume). This is the workhorse ad. Stable week-over-week with slight improvements across the board. Frequency at 2.4 is manageable but worth monitoring.

3. **UGC Talking Head — Serum Benefits** — 4.16x ROAS, $24.04 CPA, 52 conversions. Solid and consistent. Frequency ticking up from 1.8 to 2.1 — still healthy but trending in the wrong direction.

### Declining / Fatiguing

1. **Bold Billboard — 40% Off Launch** — 🔴 Revenue dropped 34% ($3,200 → $2,100) while spend held flat. ROAS crashed from 3.37x to 2.14x. CPA ballooned to $46.67. Frequency at 3.2 (was 2.7). Conversions dropped from 32 to 21. **This ad is fatigued.** Recommended action: pause within 48 hours. The discount offer may have saturated its audience. Consider refreshing with a new hook or testing a different offer angle.

2. **Carousel Education — Ingredients** — 🟡 Revenue dropped 27% ($1,100 → $800). CTR fell from 2.43% to 2.00%. Frequency rising to 2.8. Conversions down from 11 to 8. CPA at $50.00 is the highest in the account. This ad is losing steam. **Recommended action:** test a new lead slide with a stronger hook. The educational angle may need a more curiosity-driven opening.

### New Creative

1. **Meme Native — Skincare Humor** — Launched this week. $200 spend, $450 revenue (2.25x ROAS). Early CTR of 5.00% is the highest in the account — the native format is stopping the scroll. CVR at 1.00% is low, which is expected for a top-of-funnel format. 9 conversions on low spend. **Early signal: promising.** The high CTR suggests the creative is engaging. Give it another week of data before scaling. Consider pairing it with a retargeting ad to capture the high click volume.

## Anomaly Flags

1. **ROAS-spend divergence** — Spend up 12.9% but ROAS down 8.2%. The account is spending more to get less efficient returns. Primary cause: the Bold Billboard ad is consuming $980/week at 2.14x ROAS, dragging down the blended number. Reallocating this budget to the Testimonial Card or Before/After would immediately improve overall efficiency.

2. **Bold Billboard revenue cliff** — 34% revenue drop in one week with stable impressions. This isn't a delivery issue — it's creative fatigue. The audience has seen this ad too many times (frequency 3.2) and is no longer responding.

## Recommendations

1. **Pause Bold Billboard within 48 hours** — It's burning $980/week at 2.14x ROAS with declining metrics across the board. Reallocate budget to Testimonial Card and Before/After.

2. **Scale Testimonial Card by 30-40%** — At 5.07x ROAS and 1.6 frequency, this ad has the most headroom. Increase daily budget gradually ($25-30/day increments) and monitor CPA.

3. **Refresh Carousel Education lead slide** — Test a new Slide 1 with a curiosity hook (e.g., "The ingredient dermatologists won't shut up about") instead of the current educational opening. Keep slides 2-5 the same.

4. **Let Meme Native run one more week** — Don't scale yet. The 5% CTR is exciting but CVR is low. If CVR doesn't improve by next week, consider testing a stronger CTA or product-focused landing page.

5. **Prepare 2-3 new creatives to replace Bold Billboard** — Suggest testing: a new UGC format with a result-first hook, a problem→solution static ad targeting the Researcher persona, or a founder story video. Use the Testimonial Card's social proof angle as a starting framework since that's clearly resonating.
